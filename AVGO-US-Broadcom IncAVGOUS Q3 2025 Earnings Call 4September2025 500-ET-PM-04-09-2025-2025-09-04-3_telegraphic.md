**Joshua Buchalter (TD Cowen) — Q&A**

- Asked: scale-up Ethernet momentum vs. UALink and PCIe; significance of Tomahawk Ultra for lower latency; size of scale-up Ethernet opportunity over next year?

**Hock Tan response:**
- Broadcom's Ethernet solutions are disaggregated from AI accelerators — treated separately from XPUs; believes in open, interoperable networking
- XPU customers develop interfaces compatible with Ethernet; Broadcom openly enables Ethernet as networking protocol of choice with XPUs
- For GPU-based closed systems: less Ethernet adoption except at hyperscalers who architect GPU clusters separately from networking
- Hyperscalers buy significant Ethernet switches for scale-out; scale-across expected to drive even more disaggregated Ethernet adoption
- For XPU customers: all Ethernet
- Latency concern (especially scale-up) addressable with Ethernet; switch tuning can achieve sub-250 nanosecond latency — better than NVLink and InfiniBand
- Ethernet advantage: 25+ years of proven technology; open source; multiple vendors compete; no need for proprietary protocols

**Christopher Rolland (Susquehanna) — Q&A**

**Hock Tan response:**
- Ethernet is well-proven, well-known to hyperscaler architects designing AI infrastructure; logical choice
- Development of separate individualized protocols (UALink, PCIe for networking) questioned; Ethernet already does the job
- Latency gap addressable: sub-250 ns achievable with Ethernet switches; Broadcom not the only one who can do this
- Ethernet is open source; competition exists — hyperscalers value vendor optionality
- XPU development: all designed with Ethernet-compatible interfaces
- On XPU competition: competition exists; Broadcom strategy is to out-invest and out-innovate
- Broadcom advantages: first-mover in XPU/ASIC model; large IP portfolio including SerDes; best-in-class packaging; low-power design
- Continues to invest heavily to maintain lead
