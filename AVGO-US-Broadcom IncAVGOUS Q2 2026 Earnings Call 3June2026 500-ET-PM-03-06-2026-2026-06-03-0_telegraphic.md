**Hock E. Tan – President, CEO & Director, Broadcom Inc.**

*Q2 FY2026 Results*

- Q2 operating margin record 67%; adjusted EBITDA record 69% of revenue; above guidance
- Operating/EBITDA margins remain strong and stable despite massive AI-driven revenue scaling

*Semiconductors*
- Q2 semiconductor revenue record $15B; up 79% YoY
- AI semiconductor revenue record $10.8B; up 143% YoY; above outlook
- Networking ~40% of Q2 AI revenue
- Demand for XPUs and networking described as "insatiable"
- Q2 AI semiconductor bookings exceeded $30B vs. $10.8B shipped

*AI Semiconductor Outlook*
- H2 2026: AI semiconductor revenue expected to double vs. H1
- Q3 guidance: AI semiconductor revenue $16B; up 200%+ YoY
- FY2026 full-year AI semiconductor revenue expected $56B; up ~180% from FY2025
- FY2027 AI semiconductor revenue reiterated to exceed $100B
- FY2028 AI semiconductor revenue growth expected to continue based on six core customer initiatives

*Customer Programs*
- Google: long-term agreement signed April for multiple TPU generations and AI networking; relationship described as "strategic and very substantial"; superior technology vs. alternatives ensures sustained/growing business
- Anthropic: 2026 — providing 1GW+ TPU-based compute access; April agreement for additional 5GW next-gen TPU-based compute beginning 2027
- OpenAI: silicon delivered; production on track late 2026; contractual commitment 1.3GW deployment in 2027; part of 10GW-by-2029 agreement announced prior year
- Meta: April partnership for multiple MTIA XPU generations; expected 3GW deployment through end 2028; initial 1GW order (XPUs + networking) received; delivery begins H2 2027
- Two other customers: shipments beginning late 2026, accelerating into 2027; purchase orders totaling $6B received to date

*Networking Technology*
- Scale-up within racks: direct-attach copper with industry-leading 200G and 400G SerDes; co-packaged copper with Ethernet and PCIe switches
- Scale-out between racks: shipping industry's only 100-terabit Ethernet switch (Tomahawk 6) for over one year; next-gen 200-terabit switch tape-out this quarter
- CPO: 1.6-terabit DSPs, CW and EML lasers — de facto industry standard
- Cross-datacenter AI clusters: Jericho3 and Jericho4 fabric solutions; enabling world's largest deployments across multiple hyperscalers

*XPV Platform*
- Strategic vision: combine Broadcom technology with strong-balance-sheet investor partners to deliver scalable compute at lowest cost/power for frontier AI labs
- Creating AI XPV platform with Apollo, Blackstone, and other leading investors; target 20GW+ compute capacity through 2028
- First tranche valued at $35B; currently being launched by Apollo

*Non-AI Semiconductors*
- Q2 non-AI revenue $4.2B; up 6% YoY
- Q2 bookings exceeded $6B; indication of path toward full cyclical recovery
- Broadband, server storage, enterprise networking up; partially offset by seasonal wireless decline
- Q3 forecast non-AI semiconductor revenue ~$4.5B; up 12% YoY
- Q3 total semiconductor revenue expected $20.5B; up 124% YoY

*Infrastructure Software*
- Q2 software revenue $7.2B; up 9% YoY; in line with guidance
- ARR growth sustained at 17% YoY

- VCF 9.1 deployment for on-prem cloud extremely strong driven by global server demand

**Edward Snyder – Charter Equity Research**
- Q: Anthropic and OpenAI have very large gigawatt commitments vs. older customers — signals a second wave of demand as AI hits enterprises and consumers?

**Hock Tan**
- Enterprise AI consumption still at early stage
- Most enterprise token consumption purchased via APIs from Anthropic, OpenAI, Gemini platforms
- Substantially most token consumption tied to those LLMs
- As LLMs productize frontier models (Opus 4.7, ChatGPT 5.5, Gemini 3.5), demand flows back to same compute capacity suppliers
- Enterprise/consumer demand growth drives same few LLM players, not direct XPU/GPU purchases by 100,000 companies
- Describes trajectory as "sustainable and steepening" through 2027 and 2028

- Follow-up Q: Does this open XPU access to smaller companies through hyperscaler cloud platforms?

**Hock Tan**
- Compute capacity mostly delivered as SaaS/APIs via clouds (Bedrock, Vertex, Azure, first-party)
- Demand in terms of compute capacity flows from few large frontier model developers and their products to consumers/enterprises globally
- Not coming from enterprises directly buying XPUs; early stage of direct enterprise deployment
- Most demand driven by frontier model players creating products (code assistants, engineering verticals, etc.)

**Joseph Moore – Morgan Stanley**
- Q: $30B AI bookings in quarter vs. ~$10-16B shipment rate — why so much backlog building?

**Hock Tan**
- Customers recognize long lead times required for compute: wafers, HBM, DRAM, power infrastructure, power shell all must align
- Orders placed early and in large quantities; provides Broadcom far greater visibility than typical semiconductor business
- Visibility now extends to 2028; three months prior extended only to 2027
- Backlog not due to component shortage but to need to align infrastructure elements: power, data center connectivity, nationwide inference distribution
- XPV platform created specifically to plan and build capacity for frontier model customers given this long lead time
