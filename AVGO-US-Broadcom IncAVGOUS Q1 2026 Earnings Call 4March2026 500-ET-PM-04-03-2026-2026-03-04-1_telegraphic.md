**Tom O'Malley – Barclays**

- Asked: why specifically highlight that customers staying on direct-attached copper through 400G serdes, especially as Broadcom is CPO pioneer?
- Asked (to Charlie): as more ASIC customers added, how does scale-up Ethernet develop?

**Hock E. Tan (response)**

- Direct-attached copper: lowest latency, lowest power, lowest cost for scale-up (XPU-to-XPU, GPU-to-GPU within rack/cluster domain)
- Broadcom can push copper from 100G to 200G to 400G serdes for rack-scale distances
- Scale-out uses optical — appropriate there; but scale-up should maximize copper as long as possible
- CPO is not needed now; "bright shiny object"; CPO will come in its time — not this year, possibly not next year
- Broadcom leads in CPO when it does arrive

**Charlie B. Kawwas (response)**

- Ethernet became de facto cloud standard over past two decades
- Two years ago: industry unclear on back-end network protocol for scale-out; Broadcom was clear — Ethernet; now entire industry aligned on Ethernet scale-out
- Scale-up protocol debate now at similar stage as scale-out was 3–4 years ago
- Consistently hearing Ethernet is right answer for scale-up; multiple hyperscalers and semiconductor peers announced Ethernet scale-up support last year
- Many XPU designs Broadcom is engaged on explicitly targeting Ethernet scale-up

- Majority of six customers now headed toward simultaneous training+inference chip development roadmaps
- Trend toward specialization as customers mature in XPU journey
